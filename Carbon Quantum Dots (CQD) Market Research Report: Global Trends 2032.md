Market Drivers
The surging demand for CQDs across multiple sectors is primarily driven by:
•	Growing Application in Healthcare: CQDs are increasingly used in bioimaging, drug delivery, and biosensing due to their high biocompatibility and low toxicity.
•	Advancements in Optoelectronics: Their superior luminescence properties make them ideal for LED displays, solar cells, and sensors.
•	Rising Environmental Concerns: CQDs are replacing heavy-metal-based quantum dots, offering sustainable solutions in environmental monitoring and water purification.
Market Restraints
Despite the promising growth trajectory, the CQD market faces certain challenges:
•	High Production Costs: The synthesis of high-quality CQDs remains costly, limiting large-scale adoption.
•	Scalability Issues: Achieving consistent quality and large-scale production poses significant challenges for manufacturers.
•	Regulatory Hurdles: Stringent regulatory frameworks concerning nanomaterials could impede market expansion.
